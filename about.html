About Quinn

Quinn McMath is a composer for film, tv, video games, and other forms of media. He has been developing his craft in composition for seven years through working with various ensembles ranging from full orchestras to big band jazz ensembles. Quinn seeks to provide a well suited musical texture for every project he works on. Each piece of media deserves its own soundscape and Quinn will ensure that his music conveys the perfect mood to accompany any visual.

Through living in Boston, Massachusetts, Quinn has gained experience collaborating with world class musicians in the spheres of both composition and the performing arts. In his time at Berklee College of Music pursuing a Bachelors of Fine Arts in Film Scoring, he has been successful in achieving a deep understanding of the process of film scoring. He is currently a board member of Berklee's Film Scoring Network, a group which connects professional film composers to aspiring students through interviews and scoring opportunities.

What makes Quinn's composition style unique is his extensive instrumental knowledge from years as a multi-instrumentalist. Quinn believes the best way to learn how to compose for an instrument is to be able to play it. Following this motto, he has learned and played every major wind, brass, and percussion instrument proficiently in a musical ensemble. In addition to this, he has been trained in guitar, voice, drumset, and musical theatre.

Aside from scoring, Quinn is proficient as an orchestrator, arranger, and copyist. His arrangements for wind ensembles and choir have been self published and performed in California, Hawaii, Michigan, and Massachusetts by both collegiate and professional ensembles. Most recently, he did copyist work for a production of Disney's Hunchback of Notre Dame Musical.
